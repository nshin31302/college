Dear John,

We have arrived at the end of our application review period, and I am sorry to say that we are unable to offer you a space in this year's incoming class.

Please know that our decision is in no way a statement about your ability to succeed in college, nor is it any kind of judgment of you as a person. Be proud of your accomplishments, and press on in your pursuit of higher education.

This year, admission to USC was more competitive than ever. We were unable to accommodate thousands of very strong candidates, including many students with excellent academic records, remarkable talents, outstanding character, enriching experiences, and unique perspectives. I hope you can be comforted in knowing that none of us enjoys turning away capable students.

Numbers do not tell the whole story, but I hope you find the context helpful: We received nearly 66,000 applications for admission. To enroll a class of 3,200 freshmen, we offered admission to only 11.4 percent of those who applied, or roughly 7,600 students. Of that offered admission, most achieved nearly straight-A averages and presented standardized test scores at or above the 97th percentile.

In addition to grades and test scores, we considered many other factors presented in your application materials. Ours is a qualitative process, in which opinions are weighted with facts. Your application was carefully, respectfully and thoughtfully reviewed multiple times, by many individuals.

Thank you for considering USC. We all wish you the best in your college plans.

Respectfully,


Kirk Brennan
Associate Dean
Director of Undergraduate Admission

Annual Security & Fire Safety Prospective Student Notification 2019
The University of Southern California’s Annual Security and Fire Safety Reports (ASR) for the past three years are available online at: https://dps.usc.edu/alerts/annual-report/
The Annual Security and Fire Safety Report includes Clery Act crime and fire statistics for the preceding three years for locations owned and or controlled by USC, the required policy disclosure statements and other important safety related information. A paper copy of the ASR is available on request made to Department of Public Safety Records by calling (213) 740-6000, by email to DPSRecords@dps.usc.edu or in person at DPS at 3667 South McClintock Avenue, Los Angeles, California 90089
